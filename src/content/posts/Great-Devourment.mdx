## The Day Everything Changed

What began as a typical day in Qarnivore Central quickly descended into the most catastrophic event in recorded history. The Great Devourment, as it came to be known, eliminated nearly 80% of the entire Qarnivore population in less than 24 hours.

## Scientific Analysis

The scientific community remains divided on the nature and purpose of the Great Devourment. The three prevailing theories are:

### 1. Nutritional Necessity

Some researchers believe the Snuggaloids required massive energy intake to sustain their existence in our dimension. Dr. Munch's final paper, "Quantum Nutritional Requirements of Interdimensional Entities," suggests that the comfort fields generated by Snuggaloids required periodic "refueling" through conversion of compatible biomatter.

### 2. Evolutionary Catalyst

The more controversial "Evolutionary Leap" theory proposes that the Great Devourment was actually an attempt to accelerate Qarnivore evolution. Supporters point to the fact that surviving hybrid Qarnivores exhibited enhanced capabilities and quantum sensitivity after the event.

### 3. Transcendence Initiative

The Utensil Theological Council maintains that the Devourment was a form of ascension, allowing Qarnivores to transcend their physical forms and join the Snuggaloids in the Comfort Dimension. This theory is primarily based on the expressions of bliss observed on victims' faces during conversion.

## Historical Significance

The Great Devourment marks the definitive boundary between the Snuggaloid Emergence era and the Post-Extinction Recovery period. It represents the single most significant population reduction event in recorded Qarnivor history and led to the development of the hybrid society that flourishes today.

Historians continue to study the limited records of this event, seeking to understand both its immediate causes and its long-term implications for Qarnivor-Snuggaloid relations.
