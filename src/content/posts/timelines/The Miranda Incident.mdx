## Captain's Log, Stardate 45.7.21

We've been drifting through the expanding debris field that was once the Miranda system for three weeks now. Our mission is to determine the cause of the sudden supernova of the primary sun in the miranda system, though what we've found raises more questions than it answers.

Yesterday, our sensors detected a quantum resonance signature pattern. After triangulating the source, we deployed drones into a particularly dense pocket of radiation.

What we recovered is... unsettling. Not just because of the content, but because of the contradictions. Upon our initial scan we found what seemed to be ordinary radiation signatures, but as we approached, the readings shifted. It was as if the very fabric of reality was trying to hide something from us. In the end, the drones returned without any data, but the radiation readings were off the charts. The tachyon emissions were unlike anything I've ever seen—they were decaying in reverse and the radiation was sublimating in a manner that was breaking the second law of thermodynamics—instead of decaying, it was reconstituting itself into order—and traveling in the wrong direction through time.

I'm documenting everything here for the Temporal Archives, though I expect half of this will be classified before we even reach port. The irony isn't lost on me that I'm essentially preserving fragments of something that someone went to extraordinary lengths to erase from existence.

## The Temporal Anomaly

The crew has begun acting strangely—forgetful even. No one can explain the dreams. Even I have them, though they are vague, and each crew member has a different account. It cuts off abruptly at what I believe is the exact moment the temporal preservation field began to collapse. Three elderly men playing cards, and the arrival of the individual in the fedora and mask. What happens next is the ordering of "a Bloody Mary, no pickles, make it a double"—apparently a trigger phrase for some kind of extraction protocol—then we wake up.

My xenolinguistics officer insists the phrase has no special properties, but every time she says it in our lab, the recovered artifacts vibrate slightly. I've forbidden anyone from speaking it aloud.


## Personal Log Addendum: The Witnesses

Last night, I dreamt I was in that saloon again, playing cards with those old men. I've never had a dream so vivid. When I woke, I could smell the old cigar smoke and machine oil. It's happening to the rest of the crew too. Exposure to the radiation in the sector is doing something to us. The ship's counselor says it's just stress, but I'm not so sure.

We have begun to compile our logs in a shared document, and I can vividly see the movements of the old men staring back at me from the descriptions the crew provides. I notice the changes in the text based on different accounts from the crew too. It's as if we're all experiencing the same event from different perspectives, yet none of us can remember it clearly. The ship's AI has flagged this as a potential temporal anomaly.

## Personal Log Addendum: The Mechanical Observer

We have had a breakthrough in our investigation. It appears the automated service unit was bluetooth connected to the saloon's mainframe. The data was dumped and forwarded to metadata scrapers, and a copy of it has been recovered from the data transmission center on one of the icy outer planetoids in the outer asteroid debris cloud. The transmission center itself was damaged beyond repair as a result of the shockwave from the supernova, but the data was intact up to the moment the transmission was cut off. 

What is most interesting is the data stream in the logs is much longer than it should be—1,342 times longer than the time it should have taken to transmit the data. The data stream is also heavily encrypted, and the encryption key appears to be a recipe for a Bloody Mary. Most puzzling of all, the transmission continued after the initial shockwave from the sun passed over the planet—which should not be possible.

## The Cosmic Traffic Jam

Last night, our long-range sensors detected something unprecedented—the final transmissions from the planet are finally getting picked up by stations in the surrounding light cone as the data expands outward faster than the cast off matter from the stars. The doomed inhabitants, unable to use FTL to escape the expanding energy shockwave, transmitted their final communications. Among these is what appears to be a queue of ships suspended in the upper atmosphere where Miranda once orbited. When we adjusted our sensors to a quantum resonance frequency of 1,342 Hz, we captured this fragmented communication:

## Captain's Final Notes

Tomorrow we leave the Miranda debris field, but I'm not certain we're truly leaving it behind. These fragments have changed us. Last night in the mess hall, I caught myself ordering "a Bloody Mary, no pickles"—I stopped before completing the phrase. The bartender looked at me strangely and asked if I meant a tomato juice. I said yes.

When he served it, I could have sworn it glowed slightly. And for 3.7 seconds, I understood everything—the nature of time, the purpose of the Transtemporal Singularity Conflict, the significance of the Perfect Mary. Then it faded, leaving only the taste of celery salt on my tongue.

I've ordered all of these findings sealed upon our return to port. Some knowledge is too dangerous. Some recipes should remain lost.
